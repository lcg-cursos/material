About This Course

Philosophy, Approach, and Learning Design

Learn more about the course design, pedagogical approach, and what makes this learning experience unique
Author

Heladia Salgado

1 Course Overview

This course is designed to provide a comprehensive, engaging, and effective learning experience. We combine theoretical knowledge with practical applications to ensure you can immediately apply what you learn.

NoteCourse Philosophy

Our approach is built on three core principles:

  1. Learn by Doing: Theory is important, but practice is essential
  2. Progressive Complexity: Build knowledge incrementally
  3. Real-World Relevance: Every concept connects to practical applications

2 Learning Design

2.1 Pedagogical Approach

Active Learning

Engage with content through interactive exercises, discussions, and projects rather than passive consumption.

Scaffolded Learning

Start with fundamentals and progressively build to advanced topics with clear learning pathways.

Constructivist Approach

Build new knowledge on existing understanding through guided discovery and problem-solving.

Collaborative Learning

Learn from peers through discussions, group projects, and shared problem-solving.

2.2 Learning Cycle

Each chapter follows a structured learning cycle:

  1. Introduction

Set context and learning objectives. Activate prior knowledge.

  1. Concept Development

Present new ideas with clear explanations and examples.

  1. Guided Practice

Work through examples with support and scaffolding.

  1. Independent Application

Apply concepts independently through exercises and projects.

  1. Reflection & Assessment

Consolidate learning and assess understanding.

4 Assessment Philosophy

4.1 Formative Assessment

TipContinuous Feedback

We use frequent, low-stakes assessments to:

  • Monitor understanding in real-time
  • Identify areas needing additional support
  • Provide opportunities for improvement
  • Reduce assessment anxiety

4.2 Summative Assessment

Final assessments focus on:

  • Demonstrating comprehensive understanding
  • Applying knowledge to novel situations
  • Creating original solutions
  • Reflecting on learning journey

6 Course Development

6.1 Design Process

This course was developed following best practices in instructional design:

  1. Needs Analysis: Identified learner needs and industry requirements
  2. Learning Objectives: Defined clear, measurable outcomes
  3. Content Development: Created engaging, accurate content
  4. Peer Review: Expert validation and feedback
  5. Pilot Testing: Refined based on student feedback
  6. Continuous Improvement: Ongoing updates and enhancements

6.2 Evidence-Based Practices

Our design incorporates research-backed strategies:

  • Spaced repetition for long-term retention
  • Retrieval practice for stronger memory
  • Interleaving of topics for better understanding
  • Elaborative interrogation for deeper learning
  • Self-explanation for conceptual mastery
